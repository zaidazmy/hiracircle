The why

Built for the habit,
not the library.

A few years ago, someone told me he’d recited his evening azkar every day for years — without missing once. Not because he’s unusually disciplined. Because it had simply become part of who he is, like brushing his teeth.

That stayed with me. In busy lives, morning and evening dua gets pushed to “later” — and later rarely comes.

I looked for an app built around that habit. I found plenty of azkar apps. They were libraries of duas, not routines. Ma’thurat Global is built around one core feature: helping you build the wird — with streaks — until showing up every day is simply part of who you are.

For everyone

In the language you
actually think in.

Most azkar resources explain meaning in Arabic, English, or Urdu. If Tamil or Sinhala is the language you think in, you’re usually left out of that understanding.

Ma’thurat Global carries translations in eight languages, sourced from real scholarship rather than AI guesses. The Arabic recitation stays exactly as it is — the translation is only there to help you understand it.
